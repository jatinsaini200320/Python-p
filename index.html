Why Use optimised amount of fertilizer

Fertilizers are used to provide plants with essential nutrients for healthy growth, which can lead to increased crop yields and improved food quality. Fertilizers can also help with the following: Soil health: Fertilizers can increase soil fertility, which promotes plant growth. They can also improve soil structure by feeding microorganisms, which in turn make nutrients available to plants. Nutrient deficiencies: Fertilizers can correct plant nutrient deficiencies. For example, phosphorus from fertilizers can help plants expand their roots and produce seeds more quickly. Plant resilience: Fertilizers can help plants become more resilient against harmful plant pathogens and pests. Soil adjustment: Fertilizers can be used to adjust the acidity level of soil. Increased productivity: Fertilizers can help increase agricultural productivity. Food security: Fertilizers can help ensure food security.

Fact

A fertilizer is defined as a chemical substance that contains necessary nutrients such as nitrogen, potassium and phosphorus to make the soil fertile. The most common examples of fertilizers used by farmers are urea, potash and ammonium sulphate.
